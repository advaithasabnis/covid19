The doubling time is the time (in days) it will take to double the number of total cases or deaths. Higher doubling time is better! For example, Canada showed a doubling time of 2.8 days at the start of the spread meaning that the total number of cases were doubling approx. every 3 days. We are flattening the curve if the doubling time keeps increasing!

The logarithmic graphs of cumulative cases are used to show the growth rate i.e. the rate at which total cases grow every day. On a logarithmic scale, exponential growth looks like a straight rising line. If a region is slowing the spread, you will see the curve bending down.
