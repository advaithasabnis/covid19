Are We Flattening The Curve?

The logarithmic graphs of cumulative cases (or deaths) are used to show the growth rate i.e. the rate at which total cases (or deaths) grow every day. On a logarithmic graph, exponential growth (growth at a fixed % per day) looks like a straight rising line. If a country is slowing the spread, you will see the curve angling down and the daily percentage growth in cases start to drop.

The doubling time is the time in days it will take to double the number of total cases at the current rate (average over the last 5 days). Higher doubling time is better!

For comparison purposes, country curves are aligned to day 0, the day the country reached 1000 total cases , even though countries reached this milestone on different calendar dates.
